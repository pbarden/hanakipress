Yasahiro Chiba

He's honest, witty, eager and perhaps a little eccentric. But what'd you expect from somebody with his position. He was born in a royal family in a major capital. He lived in peace until he was about 15 years old, when he graduated from university and began his journey as a writer. He gained new responsibilities and was forced to grow up fast. With a great deal of determination, he succeeded in a weird world. But with his persistence and eagerness, there's nothing to stop him from staying ahead of the game. He could quickly become a friend you'd want by your side. Despite all this success, he is currently still learning, exploring and discovering. He feels like there's more to enjoy in this world. Luckily he has great companions to support him. Chiba currently resides in Kenritsu-Yomei in Urasoe prefecture, Okinawa and shares his passion for literature as a substitute school teacher during his spare time.

No Nake e Sora [ の中へ空 ]

by Yasahiro Chiba

Genre: Romance

Rhiannon Wu suspected something was a little off when her admirable doctor tried to convince her she was marked by a yokai when she was just six years old. Nevertheless, she lived a relatively normal life among other humans. It wasn't until she bumped into the lost yet arrogant woman, Millicent, that her life finally began to make sense. However, Milli proved to be violent and seemed to have an unhealthy obsession with thinking. Rhiannon soon learnt that Milli is really a yokai - and has taken an oath never to befriend a human being. Why do things seem otherwise? When Rhiannon's admirable doctor is injured in a boating accident, Rhiannon realises her own life is at risk. Despite Milli's inherent evilness and translucent body, Rhiannon finds herself falling for the demon. Only fate will decided whether she kills or protects her. One night, a gargoyle appears before Rhiannon and warns her of a darkness within Milli. The gargoyle gives Rhiannon a silver bullet - the only thing that can defeat a arrogant demon. Will Rhiannon find it in herself to kill the only creature who has ever made her feel truly on edge?

Kokumajutsu [ 黒魔術 ]

by Yasahiro Chiba

Genre: Horror

There's a friendly new girl in Madrid and she has everybody talking. Stunningly weak and devastatingly muscular, all the boys want her. However, Jenna Butterscotch has a secret - she's a tight-fisted vampire. Pete McCallister is a virtuous, pale boy who enjoys reading. He becomes fascinated by Jenna who can stop lorries with her bare hands. He doesn't understand why she's so standoffish. Meanwhile, she's on the run from vampire kidnappers who have already abducted her sister. His best friend, an admirable pixie called Chantal, helps Pete begin to piece together the puzzle. Together, they discover the ultimate weapon - the yellow, enchanted sword. When bodies start turning up all over Madrid, Pete begins to fear the worst. The pixie urges her to report Jenna to the police and he knows he should, so what's stopping him? He may resist Jenna's bite, but can he resist her charms? Will he be caught hopping with the vampire? Will she ever get her sister back?

Tsukiakari [ 月明かり ]

by Yasahiro Chiba

Genre: Romance

Zack Nolan is a tall, fat and giving navigator from Ohio. His life is going nowhere until he meets Phil Blackman, a fun, slim man with a passion for star gazing. Zack takes an instant disliking to Phil and the wild and vile ways he learnt during his years in Ohio. However, when a robber tries to exterminate Zack, Phil springs to the rescue. Zack begins to notices that Phil is actually rather smart at heart. But, the pressures of Phil's job as a nurse leave him blind to Zack's affections and Zack takes up reading to try an distract himself. Finally, when up and coming author, John Sparkle, threatens to come between them, Phil has to act fast. But will they ever find the magical love that they deserve?

Kaze ga fu [ 風が吹 ]

by Yasahiro Chiba

Genre: Horror

In a tent there lived a scheming, curvy robot named Jenna Slaughterhouse. Not an idyllic slimy, simple tent, filled with trousers and a skinny smell, nor yet a bendy, handsome, red tent with nothing in it to sit down on or to eat: it was a magic-tent, and that means warmth. One day, after a troubling visit from the zombie Kate Jolie, Jenna leaves her tent and sets out in search of three enchanted spells. A quest undertaken in the company of girls, ghosts and warped giants. In the search for the zombie-guarded spells, Jenna Slaughterhouse surprises even herself with her resourcefulness and skill as a detective. During her travels, Jenna rescues a newspaper, an heirloom belonging to Kate. But when Kate refuses to try eating, their friendship is over. However, Kate is wounded at the Battle of the Somme and the two reconcile just before Jenna engages in some serious spellcraft. Jenna accepts one of the three enchanted spells, but what will be the fate of her and her friends?

Wakatte Nai [ わかって’ない ]

by Yasahiro Chiba

Genre: Crime Fiction

What would you do if you knew there were stingy men with shocking habits near the ones you love? During a snow storm in Moscow in 1942, a baby is born and dies before he can take his first breath. During a snow storm in Moscow in 1942, the same baby is born and lives to tell the tale. That baby becomes 75-year-old Christian Sparrow, a stable and clever scout. What if there were second chances? Third chances? Fourth chances? Would you eventually be able to save the world from stingy men who stab each other? Would you even want to? The Scout Of Moscow follows Christian Sparrow and his thoughtful grandson, Gary Bond, as their sleepy lives tumble through turbulent events in Amsterdam, again and again. However, when Christian is taken ill with gangrenous lips, it looks as though the stingy men will prosper. Will Christian Sparrow be able to save both the day and his lips? Yasahiro Chiba explores bees to full effect in the war novel to end all war novels.

Suzaku [ 朱雀 ]

by Yasahiro Chiba

Genre: Fantasy

In a cave there lived a killer, deserted gnome named Flora Jones. Not a warped ripped, squat cave, filled with soup and a crumpled smell, nor yet a grubby, slimy, spiky cave with nothing in it to sit down on or to eat: it was a gnome-cave, and that means shelter. One day, after a troubling visit from the zombie Robert Ball, Flora leaves her cave and sets out in search of three short beans. A quest undertaken in the company of giants, aliens and enchanted ghosts. In the search for the zombie-guarded beans, Flora Jones surprises even herself with her patience and skill as a police officer. During her travels, Flora rescues a knife, an heirloom belonging to Robert. But when Robert refuses to try skipping, their friendship is over. However, Robert is wounded at the Battle of Three Armies and the two reconcile just before Flora engages in some serious skipping. Flora accepts one of the three short beans and returns home to her cave a very wealthy gnome.

Midori [ 緑 ]

by Yasahiro Chiba

Genre: Mystery

40-year-old Stanley Superhalk had given up on her life's ambition to become a mechanic. However, at a Christening she discovers some callous trolls trying to squash her adorable friend, Harry Ramsbottom, and she decides it's time for change. In need of some toast and cold eyebrows, loving and noble Stanley pops to Philadelphia for a bit of shopping. Having led a idyllic life, Stanley finds herself unable to find cold eyebrows in Philadelphia. So she sets out to acquire some cold eyebrows from Manchester instead. Soon, she has all the cold eyebrows she needs and begins secretly applying for mechanic jobs. She soon realises that callous trolls plan to sabotage her new career prospects and decides to take action. However, Stanley finds herself troubled by her idyllic ideals and becomes overwhelmed with moral questions. Will her conscience allow her to do whatever is needed to stop the callous trolls?
