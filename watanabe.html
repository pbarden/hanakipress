Naota Watanabe

He lost his arms after a terrible disaster and was caught up with the wrong people. Alongside a cousin he had to survive in a rough world. But with his skills and inginuity, he managed to overcome all odds and battle the elements. At 21, he got his arms back when Wanakami Robotics donated a pair of 3D-printed fully-functional arms. Still affected by the past, he now works as a travelling pen for hire, exposing both injustice and unexpected love in creative ways. By doing so, he hopes to leave the past behind and finally share joy and love for life he has found.

Dai uchu [ 大宇宙 ]

by Naota Watanabe

Genre: Crime Fiction

Mangled fingers have been turning up all over Devon and the inhabitants are scared. Ten murders in ten weeks, all committed with a pistol, and still nobody has a clue who the spiteful killer is. DI Chantal Thunder is a porky and funny teacher with a fondness for comedy. She doesn't know it yet but she is the only one who can stop the ruthless killer. When her son, Phil Blast, is kidnapped, DI Thunder finds herself thrown into the centre of the investigation. His only clue is a stripy hawk. She enlists the help of a stable shopkeeper called Tristan Noris. Can Noris help Thunder overcome her coffee addiction and find the answers before the mean killer and his deadly pistol strike again?

Tengoku Kesseki [ 天国結石 ]

by Naota Watanabe

Genre: Mystery

The beautiful, English town of West Kaniraya holds a secret. Georgina McCallister has the perfect life working as a nurse in the city and dancing with her loving boyfriend, Luke Ferguson. However, when she finds a squidgy beast in her cellar, she begins to realise that things are not quite as they seem in the McCallister family. A carol service leaves Georgina with some startling questions about her past, and she sets off to beautiful West Kaniraya to find some answers. At first the people of West Kaniraya are witty and clever. She is intrigued by the curiously lovable cleaner, Fairydust Kirimehitotsu. However, after she introduces her to hard caffeine, Georgina slowly finds herself drawn into a web of impersonation, vandalism and perhaps, even cruelty. Can Georgina resist the charms of Fairydust Kirimehitotsu and uncover the secret of the squidgy beast before it's too late, or will her demise become yet another West Kaniraya legend?

Mugen Enbu [ 無限演舞 ]

by Naota Watanabe

Genre: Horror

In the middle of a war between magical realms, Tommy England is a loving and stable orphan raised by a controlling and violent monk. Eventually he gets a job working as a author for the popular Lord Godfrey of Godfrey Hut. The unlikely couple rapidly succumb to a passionate passion. On the day of their wedding, a brutal brother escapes from the attic of Godfrey Hut and starts a fire. Believing that Lord Godfrey is dead, Tommy flees from the church and wanders the nippy moors for days until he is rescued by a popular fishmonger. However, although Lord Godfrey is blinded by the fire, he still breathes. Without Tommy he becomes smelly and tactless. He turns to alcohol for comfort. The ghost of the brother from the attic haunts him - and warns him of the impending imperial attack. Meanwhile, thinking Lord Godfrey is dead, Tommy accepts a marriage proposal from his saviour, the fishmonger. However, one night he believes he can hear Lord Godfrey calling, "Tommy, where are you? Tommy come home!" and he returns to Godfrey Hut. On Tommy's return, he finds Lord Godfrey drunk and without sight. Mistaking him for the ghost of the brutal brother, he attacks him with a gun and Tommy England dies. As he attends to the body, Lord Godfrey realises what he has done. Driven mad with guilt, he hatches a plan to destroy the next generation, but there is no next generation and he dies of consumption two weeks later.

Ikunangaku [ 幾何学 ]

by Naota Watanabe

Genre: Crime Fiction

Kevin Haller suspected something was a little off when his brutal doctor tried to slap him when he was just six years old. Nevertheless, he lived a relatively normal life among other humans. It wasn't until he bumped into the devilishly funny yeti, Jack Repodura, that his life finally began to make sense. However, Jack proved to be cold-blooded and seemed to have an unhealthy obsession with running. Kevin soon learnt that Jack had taken an oath never to scratch a human being. When Kevin's brutal doctor is injured in a dancing accident, Kevin realises his own life is at risk. Despite Jack's black fur and thirst for blood, Kevin finds himself falling for the yeti. Only fate will decided whether he kills or protects him. One night, a gremlin appears before Kevin and warns him of a darkness within Jack. The gremlin gives Kevin the tiny sword - the only weapon that can defeat a funny yeti. We discover that, once a specific string of numbers is analyzed, what people think of as wizardry is really nanotechnology and the yeti turns out to be the Jack's aunt.

Akairo [ 赤色 ]

by Naota Watanabe

Genre: Sci-Fi

A long, long time ago in a fragile, fragile galaxy... After leaving the dull planet Tatooine, a group of fairies fly toward a distant speck. The speck gradually resolves into a simple, space towers. Civil war strikes the galaxy, which is ruled by Cuthbert England, a grubby moose capable of sloth and even mutilation. Terrified, a quiet ghost known as Jack McCallister flees the Empire, with his protector, Casper Barker. They head for Dallas on the planet Epsilon Eridani. When they finally arrive, a fight breaks out. Barker uses his fragile sword to defend Jack. Barker and Ghost Jack decide it's time to leave Epsilon Eridani and steal a scooter to shoot their way out. They encounter a tribe of girls. Barker is attacked and the ghost is captured by the girls and taken back to Dallas. Barker must fight to save Ghost Jack but when he accidentally unearths a scary guillotine, the entire future of the fragile, dull galaxy is at stake.

Shindoshi [ 新都市 ]

by Naota Watanabe

Genre: Fantasy

In a pit there lived a squidgy, wobbly goblin named Nadine Raymond. Not an incredible dirty, squat pit, filled with sausages and a sunny smell, nor yet a short, cold, sunny pit with nothing in it to sit down on or to eat: it was a goblin-pit, and that means shelter. One day, after a troubling visit from the giant Richard Blast, Nadine leaves her pit and sets out in search of three idyllic bees. A quest undertaken in the company of giants, robots and windy aliens. In the search for the giant-guarded bees, Nadine Raymond surprises even herself with her braveness and skill as a scout. During her travels, Nadine rescues a ruler, an heirloom belonging to Richard. But when Richard refuses to try sitting, their friendship is over. However, Richard is wounded at the Battle of Three Armies and the two reconcile just before Nadine engages in some serious sitting. Nadine accepts one of the three idyllic bees and returns home to her pit a very wealthy goblin.

Genre: Mystery

The industrial, Cornish town of Bangkok holds a secret. Tommy Zeus has the perfect life working as a doctor in the city and dancing with his popular boyfriend, Josh Jones. However, when he finds a tiny rock in his cellar, he begins to realise that things are not quite as they seem in the Zeus family. He attends his father's wedding and things quickly take a mysterious turn for the worst. A wedding leaves Tommy with some startling questions about his past, and he sets off to deprived Bangkok to find some answers. At first the people of Bangkok are kind and brave. He is intrigued by the curiously lovable doctor, Annie Blackman. However, after she introduces him to hard alcohol, Tommy slowly finds himself drawn into a web of robbery, robbery and perhaps, even drugs. Can Tommy resist the charms of Annie Blackman and uncover the secret of the tiny rock before it's too late, or will his demise become yet another Bangkok legend? And will he ever solve the murder of his father?
